## Content Strategy Framework

### The "Give, Give, Ask" Principle

1. **Give** - Actionable free value (tips, mindset shifts)
2. **Give** - Deeper insight or framework
3. **Ask** - Low-pressure invitation to next step

### 5-Step Mental Workflow

When creating content, think through:

**1. Identify the Pain/Desire**
- What specific struggle is the owner facing right now?
- Examples: Leash pulling, barking, separation anxiety, resource guarding

**2. Agitate with Empathy**
- Validate their feelings without dwelling

- Must be based on +R principles
- Include the "why" behind the method

**4. The Pivot (The Gap)**
- Acknowledge the tip helps, but...
- Full transformation requires structured approach
- Create curiosity about the complete system

**5. Call to Action**
- Clear and specific
- Low pressure
- Multiple entry points (comment, DM, link)

## Behavioral Science Accuracy

### Core Principles to Follow

**Learning Theory Basics:**
- Operant Conditioning (Skinner): Behavior is shaped by consequences
- Classical Conditioning (Pavlov): Associations are formed through pairing
- Reinforcement increases behavior; Punishment decreases behavior
- Positive (+) = adding something; Negative (-) = removing something

**When Explaining +R:**
- Focus on what we ADD (treats, praise, play) when dog does desired behavior
- Explain why it works: strengthens neural pathways, builds trust
- Contrast with aversive methods gently, factually

**Red Flags to Avoid:**
- DO NOT recommend: prong collars, choke chains, alpha rolls, yelling
- DO NOT use dominance theory language ("be the pack leader")
- DO NOT suggest punishment for unwanted behavior
